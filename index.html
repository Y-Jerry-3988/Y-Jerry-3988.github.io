About

I am a Ph.D. student in the School of Computer Science at Shanghai Jiao Tong University (SJTU), advised by Prof. Guangtao Xue and Prof. Erci Xu. I received my B.Eng. from the IEEE Honor Class, School of Electronic Information and Electrical Engineering (SEIEE), SJTU (2018–2022), pursued my M.S. under Prof. Xue (2022–2024), and have been working toward my Ph.D. since 2024.

My research sits at the intersection of cloud storage systems and large language model (LLM) inference frameworks. On the storage side, I am interested in how cloud providers rethink the role of local storage — its performance, reliability, and economics — as the boundary between compute and storage keeps shifting. On the systems-for-AI side, I work on making LLM inference faster and cheaper by co-designing the runtime with the underlying storage and memory hierarchy.

My recent work Here, There and Everywhere received the Best Paper Award at USENIX FAST 2026.

Research Interests

  • Cloud storage systems — local storage, Elastic Block Storage, storage stacks, and hardware offloading.
  • LLM inference systems — KV-cache management, serving frameworks, and the memory / storage hierarchy for inference.
  • Storage for AI — bridging distributed storage and modern AI workloads.
