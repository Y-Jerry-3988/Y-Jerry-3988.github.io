About

I am a graduate student at Shanghai Jiao Tong University. My research sits at the intersection of cloud storage systems and large language model (LLM) inference frameworks. On the storage side, I am interested in how cloud providers rethink the role of local storage — its performance, reliability, and economics — as the boundary between compute and storage keeps shifting. On the systems-for-AI side, I work on making LLM inference faster and cheaper by co-designing the runtime with the underlying storage and memory hierarchy.

My recent work Here, There and Everywhere received the Best Paper Award at USENIX FAST 2026.

Research Interests

  • Cloud storage systems — local storage, Elastic Block Storage, storage stacks, and hardware offloading.
  • LLM inference systems — KV-cache management, serving frameworks, and the memory / storage hierarchy for inference.
  • Storage for AI — bridging distributed storage and modern AI workloads.
